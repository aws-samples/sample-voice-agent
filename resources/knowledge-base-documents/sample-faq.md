TROUBLESHOOTING

Q: My printer keeps disconnecting from the network. What should I try?
A: Printer network connectivity is one of the most common issues we see. Here are some steps to try before scheduling a service call. First, restart both the printer and your router by unplugging them for 30 seconds and plugging them back in. Second, check that the printer is connected to the same network as your computer -- many printers have a small display or status page that shows the current network connection. Third, try printing a network configuration page from the printer itself to verify it has a valid IP address. If the printer is connected via Wi-Fi, try moving it closer to the router or switching to a wired ethernet connection. If none of these steps resolve the issue, the problem may be a failing network adapter in the printer, and we recommend scheduling an on-site repair visit.

Q: My internet connection is slow. What could be causing this?
A: Slow internet can have many causes. Start by running a speed test at speedtest.net to verify your actual speeds versus what your ISP provides. If speeds are significantly lower than expected, try these steps. First, restart your modem and router. Second, check if other devices on the network are using bandwidth -- streaming, large downloads, or cloud backups can slow things down. Third, if you are on Wi-Fi, try connecting directly to the router with an ethernet cable to rule out wireless interference. Fourth, check for firmware updates on your router. If the problem persists, it may be a configuration issue with your network equipment, and our network setup service can help optimize your setup.

Q: My computer is running very slowly. What should I do?
A: A slow computer usually indicates one of a few common problems. First, check your storage space -- if your hard drive or SSD is more than 90 percent full, performance will degrade significantly. Second, open Task Manager on Windows or Activity Monitor on Mac and look for processes using excessive CPU or memory. Third, make sure your operating system and drivers are up to date. Fourth, run a malware scan with your antivirus software. If your computer is more than 4-5 years old, a hardware upgrade such as adding more RAM or switching to an SSD can dramatically improve performance. Our general consultation service can help you determine the most cost-effective solution.

Q: My computer won't turn on. What should I check?
A: If your computer will not power on at all, start with the basics. First, verify the power cable is securely connected to both the computer and the wall outlet. Try a different outlet to rule out a dead circuit. Second, check for any lights on the power supply or motherboard -- no lights at all usually indicates a power supply failure. Third, if the computer turns on but the screen stays black, listen for beep codes which can indicate specific hardware failures. If you hear unusual sounds like clicking from the hard drive, stop using the computer immediately to prevent data loss and contact us for an on-site repair.

Q: How do I know if my hardware is failing?
A: Common warning signs of hardware failure include: unusual noises such as clicking, grinding, or excessive fan noise; frequent crashes or blue screens; the computer running much hotter than usual; files becoming corrupted or disappearing; and the system taking much longer to boot than normal. If you notice any of these signs, we recommend backing up your important data immediately and scheduling a preventive maintenance visit or general consultation. Early detection can prevent data loss and reduce repair costs.

Q: My display is flickering or showing artifacts. What could be wrong?
A: Display issues can stem from several causes. First, check your display cable connections -- reseat the HDMI, DisplayPort, or VGA cable at both ends. Second, try a different cable if available, as cables can degrade over time. Third, update your graphics drivers to the latest version. If you are using an external monitor, try connecting it to a different port. If the flickering only occurs in certain applications, it may be a software issue. Persistent flickering or colored artifacts across all applications often indicates a failing graphics card, which may need replacement through our hardware upgrade service.

PREPARING FOR YOUR APPOINTMENT

Q: How should I prepare for a technician visit?
A: To make the most of your appointment, please have the following ready. First, back up any important data before the visit, especially if hardware work is being done. Second, make sure the technician will have physical access to the equipment that needs service. Third, prepare a list of all the issues you are experiencing, including when they started and how frequently they occur. Fourth, have any relevant warranty documentation or previous service records available. Finally, ensure a reliable power source and adequate workspace near the equipment.

Q: Do I need to be present during the appointment?
A: Yes, we require someone authorized to be present during the entire appointment. The technician may need to ask questions about the issue, get approval for additional work, or need access to passwords and accounts. If you cannot be present yourself, please designate someone who is familiar with the issues and authorized to make decisions about the service.

Q: What should I back up before a hardware upgrade?
A: Before any hardware upgrade, we strongly recommend backing up all important data. This includes documents, photos, and other personal files. Also back up your browser bookmarks, email archives, and any software license keys you may need. If possible, create a full system image backup so you can restore your entire system if needed. Our technician will take precautions during the upgrade, but having a current backup is always the safest approach.
